# Decision Log
**Package:** @gonzih/skills-executive
**Command:** /decision-log

## What it does
Structures a decision record for significant organizational choices — capturing the context, options considered, rationale, decision, owner, and review trigger. Creates an institutional memory artifact that prevents relitigating old decisions and helps new team members understand why things are the way they are.

## When to use
- After a significant strategic or operational decision
- During onboarding to explain historical choices
- When a decision is being revisited and you need to surface the original rationale

## Example output
**Decision Log Entry**

**Decision ID:** DL-2026-014
**Date:** March 15, 2026
**Decision owner:** COO
**Participants:** CEO, CFO, VP Engineering, VP People

**Decision:** Adopt a hybrid-first (not remote-first) operating model, with teams expected in-office Tuesday–Thursday starting June 2026.

**Context:** Post-COVID work arrangement policy has been informal since 2022. Headcount has grown from 40 to 140. Inconsistent practices are creating friction in cross-functional collaboration, and manager feedback indicates onboarding quality is declining.

**Options considered:**
1. Full remote — rejected; collaboration and onboarding concerns outweigh cost savings
2. Full in-office — rejected; would trigger attrition among 30%+ of current staff
3. Hybrid 3-day anchor — selected; balances flexibility with collaboration density

**Rationale:** Research and internal surveys indicate that structured co-presence 3 days/week captures most collaboration benefit. The Tuesday–Thursday anchor aligns all teams on the same days, which is critical for cross-functional work.

**Review trigger:** Re-evaluate at 12 months (March 2027) or if attrition exceeds 15% annualized in the 6 months following launch.
