# Patient Education
**Package:** @gonzih/skills-healthcare
**Command:** /patient-education

## What it does
Creates plain-language patient education materials — condition explainers, medication guides, post-procedure instructions, and lifestyle recommendations — written at a 6th-8th grade reading level. Adapts tone for pediatric, adult, or geriatric audiences on request.

## When to use
- Preparing discharge instructions
- Creating condition-specific handouts for your waiting room or patient portal
- Translating clinical language into terms patients can act on

## Example output
**Understanding Your New Diagnosis: Type 2 Diabetes**

**What is type 2 diabetes?**
Type 2 diabetes means your body has trouble using insulin — a hormone that helps sugar from food get into your cells for energy. When this doesn't work well, sugar builds up in your blood.

**Why does it matter?**
Over time, high blood sugar can affect your heart, kidneys, eyes, and feet. The good news: with the right habits and sometimes medication, most people with type 2 diabetes live full, healthy lives.

**What you can do starting today:**
- Eat smaller portions of starchy foods (bread, rice, pasta, potatoes)
- Walk 20-30 minutes most days
- Check your blood sugar as your doctor recommended
- Take your medication as prescribed — even when you feel fine

**Questions to ask at your next visit:**
- What blood sugar number should I aim for?
- Do I need to check my feet daily?
- When should I call the office?
