# Research Digest
**Package:** @gonzih/skills-healthcare
**Command:** /research-digest

## What it does
Summarizes clinical research abstracts, systematic reviews, or guideline updates into a structured digest: key findings, study design quality, clinical implications, and limitations. Designed for busy clinicians who need to stay current without reading full papers.

## When to use
- Reviewing a new clinical trial before changing prescribing practice
- Preparing a journal club presentation
- Staying current on guideline updates in your specialty

## Example output
**Research Digest — NEJM 2025**

**Study:** "Semaglutide vs. Placebo in Non-Alcoholic Steatohepatitis" (NASH-RESOLVE Trial)

**Design:** Randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled trial; n=1,200; 72-week follow-up

**Key Findings:**
- 62% of semaglutide patients achieved NASH resolution vs. 18% placebo (p<0.001)
- Fibrosis improvement (≥1 stage) in 43% vs. 22% (p<0.001)
- Mean weight loss: 11.3% semaglutide vs. 1.1% placebo

**Limitations:** Primarily white, middle-aged cohort; long-term outcomes (cirrhosis, HCC) not yet measured; industry-funded

**Clinical implication:** Strongest RCT evidence to date for a pharmacologic NASH intervention. Consider in patients with biopsy-confirmed NASH and BMI ≥27 who have not responded to lifestyle intervention.

*Clinical decisions should incorporate individual patient factors and current guidelines.*
